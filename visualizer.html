Physical vs Pixel

Your TV is 50" but only 1920px wide = 38 px/inch.

Your desk monitors are 27" but 2560px = 95 px/inch.

Moving 1 inch on the TV covers 2.5x fewer pixels than 1 inch on the desk monitors. Mouse-warp corrects for this.

Why It Feels Wrong

When you move your mouse down from the TV, the OS doesn't know your TV is physically wider than your desk monitors. It only sees pixels.

Without warp: right side of TV → middle of DP-3 (wrong). With warp: right side of TV → DP-1 (correct).
